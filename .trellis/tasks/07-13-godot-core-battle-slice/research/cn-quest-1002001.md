## Runtime ownership

The original `ZakoEmitterManager` updates every emitter independently, and each `SpawnPointImpl` holds one active zako before entering its own downtime interval. Godot now mirrors that ownership boundary:

- each emitter has one `active_serial` and independent cooldown;
- each enemy instance owns body/HP/conditions/action/state by serial;
- projectiles and funnels retain their owner serial;
- delayed player events capture enemy/funnel serials at cast time;
- the old single-enemy fields remain only a primary-instance compatibility view.

Immediate initial spawn and deterministic horizontal separation remain adapters pending the original 90-frame spawn animation and terrain spawn coordinates.
